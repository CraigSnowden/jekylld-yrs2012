What happened in 2011?

Young Rewired State 2011

Last year's Young Rewired State was a massive success, drawing in more than 150 young developers from across the country. More than 30 projects were presented to a judging panel including Andrew Sampson, Chewy Trewhella of Google, Helen Milner and Mike Butcher of TechCrunch Europe.

Read more about YRS2011 on the Young Rewired State Blog

Essentially a pedestrian heat map, showing the busiest pedestrian areas in London.

Wish the Government would do this

myNHS — James Thompson, Harry Rickards & Lewis Bryant

myNHS is a brand new way of linking patients with the NHS. The app focuses on 4 main things. Location, GPs, prescriptions and bookings.

CIO council award

Solar — Joe & Issy

This project takes data from Ofgem, strips out only photovoltaic installation information and then plots their coordinates on a Google map.

Best mobile project

Lifestyle — Alex Reed, Dylan Maryk, Simion Putina, Kevin Lewis & Kerim Hudson

Designed in order to find out more about a neighbourhood. By entering a postcode, you are able to find out about Education, Crime & Lifestyle.

Best mobile project

TrainTracker — Michael Mokrysz

A way to follow up on train punctuality, as well as to see how fast trains go on different parts of their routes.

Best data workaround

Food for Thought — Joel Murphy

An app that uses data from the Food Standards Agency to allow users to find out the hygiene ratings of food outlets.

Best data workaround

PacMap — Benn Webb & Josh Pickett

Its a game of Pacman. On an Open Street Map. Used to help find problems in the Open Street Maps dataset and for epic lulz.
